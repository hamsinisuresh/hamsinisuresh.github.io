I study how cells sense and respond to mechanical forces, integrating biophysical cues with gene regulatory programs to shape cell identity and behavior across systems.

Mechanobiology

Cells can sense and respond to mechanical cues in their environment, such as substrate shape, size, stiffness, and curvature. We developed a model to capture the different morphological states a cell adopts in response to external mechanical cues, and show that cellular patterning can happen at unexpected scales.
