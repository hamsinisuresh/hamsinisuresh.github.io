I study how cells sense and respond to mechanical forces, integrating biophysical cues with gene regulatory programs to shape cell identity and behavior across systems.

Cellular biomechanics & Mechanobiology

How do single cells respond to mechanical, chemical, and geometric cues in their microenvironments? With collaborators at the University of Cambridge, I have developed a homeostatic mechanics framework that generates cell shape, actin cytoskeletal ordering, and traction maps across different substrates, giving multiple readouts of how cells respond to their surroundings. Using this, I showed that cell alignment on micropatterns is driven by fluctuations in cell shape rather than edge contact. I also applied the framework to predict human mesenchymal stem cell differentiation across varied environments, demonstrating that cytoskeletal free-energy predicts stem cell lineage commitment better than morphological measures alone.
