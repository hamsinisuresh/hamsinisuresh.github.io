Source data and scripts used in various papers are listed below.

Cellular biomechanics & Mechanobiology

The cytoskeletal orientational order parameter provides a measure of alignment of stress-fibres in a cell. The raw data and scripts used to process actin-stained images to visualize local orientation of cytoskeleton can be found here

Matlab scripts to simulate cell shapes on adhesive stripes of different widths can be found here

Gene expression analysis

The single-cell transcriptomic data from primate MTG are available on CELLxGENE. Scripts to compare gene expression across primates can be found here. Users can also visualize the data on our interactive web server.

To assess the ability of whole mouse brain single-cell and single-nucleus atlases to serve as reference maps, we mapped neuronal and non-neuronal clusters from diverse species onto them. Scripts comparing the extent of cross-species mapping are available here

We have collected 362,772 samples from 7,767 datasets, and built high-quality, meta-analytic gene coexpression networks for 142 species. This large collection of data helps us evaluate coexpression relationships across the tree of life: from fungi to animals to plants. Check out CoCoBLAST!
