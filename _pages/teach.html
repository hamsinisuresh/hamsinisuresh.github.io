Teaching at IIT Madras

I study how gene expression differs across species at cellular resolution, with a focus on human brain evolution. Using single-cell transcriptomic atlases from five primates, I built a cross-species taxonomy of 57 homologous cell types to identify where human brain cells diverge from our closest relatives. By combining single-cell data with population-scale co-expression networks spanning 19 animals, I pinpointed a set of human-specific genes whose changed activity likely underlies the cognitive distinctiveness of the human brain. I apply this framework across systems, from brain organoids to heart evolution and plants, to reveal how changes in gene expression shape function.
